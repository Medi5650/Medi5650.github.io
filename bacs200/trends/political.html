Politcal Trends
Years ago, in 1951 Malcolm X had brought a case to the United Nations named The International Tribunal on Human Rights Abuses Against Black, Brown and Indigenous Peoples. They brought the case to a panel of jurors since millions of black, brown, and indigenous people have disappeared, been murdered, and nearly exterminated starting from 1492 to the present. Many people have also argued wrongs go beyond just the disappearances and murders such as with colonization, racism, militarism, imperialism, materialism, criminalization, patriarchy, neocolonialism, and internal colonialism. This neocolonialism refers to Prison Industrial Complex, the Military Industrial Complex, and the privatization of other social services such as healthcare. As well as Police killing, mass incarceration due to over policing in certain communities, political prisoners, environmental racism, and public health issues. As of this year the case was finally closed after hearing over 30 separate witnesses the United Nations found the United States guilty on five counts of genocide. The first being Police killings of black, brown, and indigenous people. Secondly, hyper/mass incarcerations of black, brown, and indigenous people. Third, Political incarceration of civil rights/national liberation era revolutionaries and activities, as well as present day activities. Forth, Environmental racism and its impact on black, brown, and indigenous peoples. The fifth being public health racism and its traumatic impacts. This is huge news and a big movement against the injustices here in the United States yet there has been little to no media coverage on the case. One must ask themselves why that is? Its almost like the United States does not what its own people to know that that have been found guilty of the Five counts of genocide and keep their people in the dark of what is happening in there very own country.
